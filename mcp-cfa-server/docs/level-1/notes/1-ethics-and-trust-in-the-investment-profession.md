LOS : Explain ethics

Within the field of finance/investment, **ethics** refer to a set of moral principles and **rules of conduct** that provide **guidance** to industry professionals.

Ethics represent the **beliefs** that guide the actions of investment professionals.

High ethical standards are critical to maintaining the **public’s trust** in financial markets and in the investment profession.

A **principle** is a belief or fundamental truth that serves as the **foundation** for a system of belief or behavior.

A **code of ethics** is a written set of principles that codify beliefs about **obligatory and forbidden conduct**, in community or societal groups such universities and professional organizations.

☐ Serve as a **general guide** on how community members should act.
LOS : Explain ethics

- Standards of conduct are explicit rules or standards that gives specific behaviors required of community members
- ☐ Serve as benchmarks for minimally acceptable behavior of community members and can assist in explaining code of ethics.
- ☐ To join a community, members must agree to the code of ethics and standards of conduct – usually in writing annually (or more frequent)

- The CFA Institute has six documented principles within its code of ethics. Members and candidates are obliged to observe all the principles in their line of duty and beyond.
LOS : Explain ethics

## CFA Institute Code of Ethics

## Members and candidates must:

1. Act with integrity, competence, diligence, respect, and in an ethical manner with the public, clients, prospective clients, employers, employees, colleagues in the investment profession, and other participants in the global capital markets.
2. Place the integrity of the investment profession and the interests of clients above their own personal interests.
3. Use reasonable care and exercise independent professional judgment when conducting investment analysis, making investment recommendations, taking investment actions, and engaging in other professional activities.
4. Practice and encourage others to practice in a professional and ethical manner that will reflect credit on themselves and the profession.
5. Promote the integrity and viability of the global capital markets for the ultimate benefit of society.
6. Maintain and improve their professional competence and strive to maintain and improve the competence of other investment professionals.

LOS : Describe the role of a code of ethics in defining a profession

The code of ethics communicates the shared principles and expected behavior of members and candidates.

A code of ethics constitutes a universal protocol of acceptable behavior.

The role of a code of ethics within the investment profession is to promote the integrity and viability of global capital markets for the ultimate benefit of society.

The CFA Institute Code and Standards are an example of principle-based standards; they are based on the shared principles of honesty, integrity, transparency, diligence, and placing client interests first.
LOS: Describe the need for high ethical standards in the investment industry

## Why are High Ethical Standards so Important?

- The investment industry boasts trillions of dollars in assets. As such, even a small number of unethical exchanges translates to a significant overall number.
- High standards prevent a market collapse, an event associated with devastating financial/economic effects.
- The investment industry is built upon trust, without which it would be impossible to have any form of business.
- Unethical behavior can cost you your job, reputation, and professional stature.
LOS : Explain professionalism in investment management

Investment management is a relatively new profession, which means that public understanding of its practice and codes is still developing.

However, key elements have been developed over the year:

- Investment management industry meets most of features of a profession.
- ☐ In some countries, licensing or certification may be required to practice.
- Globalization of investment management industry (mostly due to technology).
- ☐ Investment management professionals can seek cross-border opportunities or move between offices of multinational investment firms.
LOS : Identify challenges to ethical behavior

1. People tend to believe that they are ethical people and that their ethical standards are higher.
- Decision-makers generally bow to situational influences.

2. This overconfidence in one’s behavior results in a failure to consider, explicitly or implicitly, important inputs that would result in the best decision from an ethical perspective.
- Situational influences are environmental or cultural elements that shape our thinking, decision making, and behavior. For example, a junior, newly recruited CFA candidate is likely to conform to/condone actions that are inconsistent with the Code and Standards when working with colleagues who are not bound by the Code and Standards.
LOS : Distinguish between ethical and legal standards

Laws and regulations often codify ethical actions that lead to better outcomes for stakeholders and the society in general. However, legal and ethical conduct are not always the same:

LOS : Distinguish between ethical and legal standards

Some legal procedures are not considered ethical
- CFA Institute considers trading based on material non public information unethical. However, certain countries have no laws prohibiting this.

Some ethical practices may not be legal in certain jurisdictions
- A whistleblower may expose corruption or dishonesty within an organization but end up contravening local laws in the process, despite his actions being considered ethical by some.

The law is reactive, but ethical conduct is proactive.
LOS : Describe and apply a framework for ethical decision-making

- Investment professionals should possess **well-developed** set of principles when making decisions that can affect multiple stakeholders – otherwise their decisions may lead to fraudulent conduct and dissolution of the public trust.
- Employing ethical decision framework will help a decision framework provides a **wider perspective of a situation** and help in identifying aspects that may not be evident in a short-term self focused perspective.
- Ethical decision framework exists in **varying forms**.

## Framework for Ethical Decision-Making

- **Identify**
- **Consider**
- **Decide and act**
- **Reflect**

- Relevant facts: Important information available to you as well as unavailable information that may help in understanding the situation.
- Stakeholders and duties owed: Stakeholders such as clients, colleagues and market participants, and duties owed to each.
- Relevant ethical principles that may be applicable to the situation.
- Conflicts of interest inherent in a situation.
