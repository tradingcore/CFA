Changes have been **minimal** over the years:

## What are the Possible Sources of Professional Conduct Inquiries?

I. Self-disclosures via the annual Professional Conduct Statement.
II. Written complaints received by Professional Conduct staff.
III. Evidence of misconduct through public sources, such as media articles.
IV. Reports submitted by proctors detailing a candidate's conduct on exam day.
V. Analysis of exam materials after the exam and monitoring of social media by the CFA Institute.

## What Happens when an Inquiry is Initiated?

- Professional Conduct staff embark on a fact-finding mission:
- That may involve interviews with members and the complainants.

- The investigation concludes with:
- Acquittal – no disciplinary sanction.
- Issuance of a cautionary letter.
- Actual disciplinary measures.

- The member can either accept the charges or reject them, in which case the matter is escalated to the Disciplinary Review Committee for re-evaluation.

## Ethics and the Investment Industry

## Capital Market Sustainability and Actions of One

- Individuals and firms should consider the **indirect effects** of the **actions** on the whole **investment community**.
- Complying with the highest **ethical behavior** ensures the long-term health of the **whole market**.
- **Isolated** and **unrelated** decisions may be considered **individually** harmless but can cause a market crisis **in aggregate**.
- **Investment professionals** should apply a **broader context** while assessing the impact of their **decision-making** process impact on the global financial markets.
- Leaders should consider the **wider context** of market sustainability in their development and approval of corporate policies.
- **Corporate compensation** strategies should not encourage unethical behavior.

## Ethics and the Investment Industry

## Relationship between Ethics and Regulations

- Both ethics and laws and regulations limit the behavior to seek self-interest that could harm interest of others.
- However, laws and regulations guide towards ethical behavior but do not consider all unethical behavior.
- Legal behavior is what required, while ethical behavior is what is considered morally correct.
- Ethical principles covers beyond what is considered legally sufficient.
- Many regulators lack enough resources to enforce well-thought-out rules and regulations.
- Relying on compliance with laws and regulations alone is not enough to ensure ethical behavior of professionals or to develop an ethical culture in the industry.
- Individuals can evade regulatory rules for personal gain.
- Application of ethical principles caps abuse of regulations.

## Ethics and the Investment Industry

## Applying an Ethical Framework

- Laws, regulations, professional standards and code of ethics are not enough to guide ethical behavior.
- Developing an ethical framework for an internal thought process before making a decision in an important step in committing to ethical conduct.
- Given the importance of ethical behavior in discharging professional responsibilities, it is important not to make decisions based on a business outlook only.
- It crucial to consider an ethical perspective – using an ethical framework.
- Ethical framework allows investment professionals to analyze their conduct while considering inherent conflicts of interest effectively.
- Meeting high standards of ethical behavior.

## How does the CFA Institute Ensure that the Code and Standards are Effective?

Via the CFA Institute Standards of Practice Council (SPC).

The SPC is a group consisting of CFA charterholder volunteers charged with maintaining and interpreting the Code and Standards, as well as evaluate the guidance in the Handbook, to ensure that they are:

- Representative of high standards of professional conduct.
- Relevant to the changing nature of the investment profession.
- Globally applicable.
- Sufficiently comprehensive, practical, and specific.
- Enforceable.
- Testable for the CFA Program.

## Components of the Code of Ethics Members and Candidates must:

1. Act with integrity, competence, diligence, respect, and in an ethical manner with the public, clients, prospective clients, employers, employees, colleagues in the investment profession, and other participants in the global capital markets.
2. Place the integrity of the investment profession and the interests of clients above their own personal interests.
3. Use reasonable care and exercise independent professional judgment when conducting investment analysis, making investment recommendations, taking investment actions, and engaging in other professional activities.
4. Practice and encourage others to practice in a professional and ethical manner that will reflect credit on themselves and the profession.
5. Promote the integrity and viability of the global capital markets for the ultimate benefit of society.
6. Maintain and improve their professional competence and strive to maintain and improve the competence of other investment professionals.
