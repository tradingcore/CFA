An analyst studies the returns on two investments over a period of 36 months and finds that the covariance between them is significantly negative. This implies that the investment returns have:

A) no linear relationship.
B) a covariance between 0 and -1.
C) a negative correlation coefficient. ☑

### Explanation

The correlation coefficient is equal to the covariance divided by the product of the two variables' standard deviations. Because standard deviations must be positive, if the covariance is negative, so is the correlation coefficient. A negative covariance implies that the variables have a negative a linear relationship (a covariance of 0 implies there is not a linear relationship). The correlation coefficient would be between 0 and -1, not the covariance.

(Module 3.2, LOS 3.d)
