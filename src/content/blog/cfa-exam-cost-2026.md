## CFA Institute fees: the official cost

These are the unavoidable fees paid directly to CFA Institute. Numbers below reflect 2026 schedules; **always confirm on the [official CFA Program fees page](https://www.cfainstitute.org/programs/cfa/exam/fees) before registering** — windows and amounts change.

### 1. One-time enrollment fee

- **Amount:** ~USD 350
- **When paid:** Once, the first time you register for Level I.
- **Refundable:** No.

You never pay this again, even if you retake levels.

### 2. Exam registration fee (per attempt, per level)

CFA Institute uses two pricing windows for each sitting:

- **Early registration** — opens roughly 9–10 months before exam date. Cheapest pricing.
- **Standard registration** — runs until the registration deadline (~3–4 months before exam date).

| Window | Approximate fee (USD) |
|---|---|
| Early | ~\$940 |
| Standard | ~\$1,290 |

You pay this fee for **every attempt of every level**. Failing and retaking means paying again.

### 3. Total CFA Institute fees, no retakes

Best case (all early registrations, no retakes):

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Enrollment fee | \$350 |
| Level I (early) | \$940 |
| Level II (early) | \$940 |
| Level III (early) | \$940 |
| **Total** | **\$3,170** |

Standard registrations only:

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Enrollment fee | \$350 |
| Level I (standard) | \$1,290 |
| Level II (standard) | \$1,290 |
| Level III (standard) | \$1,290 |
| **Total** | **\$4,220** |

The difference between early and standard across all three levels is **~\$1,050** — meaningful money. **Plan registration aggressively.**

## Prep materials: the variable cost

This is where total spend swings the most. Three buckets:

### Free / minimal

- **CFA Institute Learning Ecosystem (LES)** — official curriculum, practice questions, mock exams. Included with registration.
- **CFA Institute mock exams** — additional mocks may be included or low-cost.
- **YouTube channels** — free, variable quality.

**Typical spend:** \$0 per level.

### Mid-tier (most common)

One major paid provider for one level. Common choices:

- **Kaplan Schweser** — condensed notes + QBank + mocks. **~\$700–\$1,500 per level**, depending on tier.
- **Wiley Efficient Learning** — similar tier and price range.
- **AnalystPrep** — generally lower priced; question bank focus. **~\$200–\$600 per level**.
- **MarkMeldrum.com** — video-led, well-regarded for L2/L3. **~\$300–\$500 per level**.
- **300Hours / Bloomberg Exam Prep / IFT** — niche but quality. Variable price.

**Typical spend:** \$400–\$1,200 per level.

### Premium / "everything"

Multiple providers, full live or recorded classes, extensive QBanks. Common at large firms with full reimbursement.

**Typical spend:** \$1,500–\$3,000 per level.

### Realistic prep cost over 3 levels

| Approach | Total over 3 levels |
|---|---|
| Free only (LES + free supplements) | \$0 |
| Mid-tier, one provider | \$1,200–\$3,600 |
| Premium stack | \$4,500–\$9,000 |

## Hidden / often-forgotten costs

- **Approved calculator** — TI BA II Plus (~\$30) or HP 12C (~\$70). Buy the BA II Plus Professional (~\$50–\$60) — it has more memory registers.
- **Travel and lodging** — if your testing center is far. For some candidates outside major cities this is non-trivial.
- **Re-takes** — every retake = another registration fee.
- **Time off work** — some candidates take a few days off before the exam.
- **Tutor or study group** — uncommon, but some pay \$50–\$150/hour for problem-area tutoring.
- **Membership** — to be awarded the charter, you must become a CFA Institute member. **Membership fees: ~\$300/year.** Plus your local CFA Society membership (~\$100–\$300/year).
- **Continuing education** — once charter awarded, ongoing PL credit recommendations.

## Total realistic spend to first charter

| Scenario | Total cost |
|---|---|
| Best case (early reg, free prep, no retakes) | **~\$3,200** |
| Common case (early reg, mid-tier prep, no retakes) | **~\$4,400–\$6,800** |
| Common case with one retake | **~\$5,500–\$8,500** |
| Premium (all standard reg, premium prep, no retakes) | **~\$7,000–\$13,000** |

Most working professionals end up in the **\$4,500–\$7,500** range over 3 years.

## Scholarships and price reductions

CFA Institute offers several scholarship programs. They reduce both the enrollment fee and the per-exam registration fee dramatically (typically to a few hundred dollars total).

| Scholarship | Eligibility |
|---|---|
| Access Scholarship | Income / financial need |
| Women's Scholarship | Identifies as female; not currently registered for the program |
| Student Scholarship | Currently enrolled at a partnered university |
| Professor Scholarship | Faculty at a partnered university |
| Regulator Scholarship | Employed at a recognized regulator |

**Application windows close months before the exam sitting.** If finances are a constraint, plan ~12 months ahead.

Details: [CFA Institute scholarships page](https://www.cfainstitute.org/programs/cfa/scholarships).

## Strategies to keep costs down

1. **Always register in the early window.** Saves \$350/level.
2. **Use the included Learning Ecosystem first** before deciding what to supplement. Many candidates over-buy.
3. **One quality prep provider is enough.** Don't stack three.
4. **Apply for scholarships early.** Most candidates don't realize how generous they are.
5. **Negotiate employer reimbursement up front.** Get the policy in writing before signing up.
6. **Pass on the first try.** Each retake is \$1,000+ and a year of delayed earnings.
