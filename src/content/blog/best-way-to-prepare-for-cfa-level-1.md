## Why most candidates underperform

The pass rate for CFA Level I sits around 37–44% in recent sittings. That number is consistent enough across years to tell you something: **the curriculum isn't impossibly hard, but most candidates approach it the wrong way**.

The three most common failure modes:

1. **Reading-heavy, practice-light.** Underlining and highlighting feels productive but doesn't move the needle. Active practice does.
2. **No phasing.** Trying to "cover everything once" right up to the exam, with no time for mocks or reinforcement.
3. **Topic blindness.** Treating all 10 topic areas as equal, ignoring weights and personal weaknesses.

This guide is the antidote: a phased, weight-aware, practice-centric plan that actually fits a working schedule.

## How many hours you actually need

CFA Institute recommends 300+ hours. The realistic distribution by background:

| Background | Hours needed |
|---|---|
| Finance / accounting professional | 250–320 |
| Engineering / quant | 280–360 |
| Business but non-finance major | 300–400 |
| Non-finance, non-quant | 350–450 |

Spread these over **5–6 months** for sustainable intensity. A typical working schedule:

- **Weekdays:** 1.5–2 hours/day (early morning or evening)
- **Weekends:** 5–7 hours total
- **Total/week:** 13–17 hours

Going below 12 hours/week stretches the timeline beyond 6 months, and retention degrades. Going above 22 hours/week sustained risks burnout.

## The four-phase framework

A realistic 5-month plan for a working professional:

| Phase | Weeks | What you do | Hours/week |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Foundation** | 1–8 | Cover heaviest topics first | 13–15 |
| **2. Coverage** | 9–14 | Finish remaining topics | 13–15 |
| **3. Reinforcement** | 15–18 | Targeted weak-area work + Ethics | 14–16 |
| **4. Mocks** | 19–22 | Full mocks + review | 18–22 |

If you have 6 months, add 2 weeks to Phase 1 (more time on FRA + Quant). If you have 4 months, compress Phases 1 and 2 into 10 weeks total, which is aggressive but doable.

## Phase 1 — Foundation (weeks 1–8)

Cover the heaviest, most prerequisite-feeling topics first while your motivation and energy are highest.

| Week | Topic | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | **Quantitative Methods** | Time value of money, probability, hypothesis testing |
| 3–5 | **Financial Statement Analysis** | Income statement, balance sheet, cash flow, ratios |
| 6 | **Economics** | Supply/demand, monetary policy, exchange rates |
| 7 | **Corporate Issuers** | Capital structure, working capital, governance |
| 8 | Buffer + light review | Catch up on slipped topics |

Why this order:

- **Quant** unlocks half of the rest of the curriculum. Time value of money appears in equity, fixed income, derivatives.
- **FRA** is the single largest topic at 11–14% and the most operational — it rewards repetition.
- **Economics** is small but non-trivial; clear it early.
- **Corporate Issuers** is light but useful before equity.

Practice question target by end of Phase 1: **~600 questions** across these topics.

## Phase 2 — Coverage (weeks 9–14)

Cover the remaining topics with the same disciplined cadence.

| Week | Topic |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | **Equity Investments** |
| 11–12 | **Fixed Income** |
| 13 | **Derivatives** |
| 14 | **Alternative Investments** + **Portfolio Management** |

Practice volume by end of Phase 2: **~1,400 questions cumulative**.

By the end of Phase 2 you've touched every topic at least once. You will not feel ready. That's normal.

## Phase 3 — Reinforcement (weeks 15–18)

This is where pass rates are made. Three streams in parallel:

1. **Topic remediation.** Take a 50-question diagnostic per topic. Anything below ~65% gets a re-study session and 100 more practice questions.
2. **Ethics deep dive.** 3 weeks of focused Ethics study — re-read the Standards, work the cases, do the official Ethics practice set. Aim for 90%+ on Ethics by week 18.
3. **Cross-topic mixed practice.** Random mixed-topic question batches of 60–90 questions, simulating exam fatigue.

Practice volume by end of Phase 3: **~2,200 questions cumulative**.

## Phase 4 — Mocks (weeks 19–22)

Mock exams are not "test what I know" — they are **the most important learning tool in the prep**.

| Week | Activity |
|---|---|
| 19 | Mock #1 (full timed). Spend 8–10 hours reviewing every wrong answer. |
| 20 | Mock #2. Same review process. Topic remediation on weak areas. |
| 21 | Mock #3. Mock #4 if you have bandwidth. |
| 22 | Mock #5. Light review of formula sheets and Ethics. **No new material.** |

Goal: **5–7 full-length mocks** in the final month, with deep review of every wrong answer (and every guessed-right answer).

The review is more important than the mock itself. For each wrong answer:

1. Write down **why** you got it wrong (concept, calculation, careless reading, ran out of time).
2. Re-read the relevant LOS.
3. Practice 5 more questions on the same sub-topic.

Candidates who do this religiously typically gain **10–20 percentage points** on subsequent mocks.

## The 5 rules that move pass rates

1. **Mocks are the curriculum.** Plan your last 4 weeks around mocks, not around new content.
2. **Practice volume > re-reading.** Read once, practice ten times.
3. **Ethics last, but treat it like 15% of your time.** The "ethics adjustment" is real on borderline scores.
4. **Don't skip the boring topics.** Corporate Issuers and Quant feel small but are easy points.
5. **Track readiness by LOS, not by chapter.** Knowing you "did" a chapter is meaningless. Knowing you can answer 80% of questions on each LOS is everything.

## What to do in the final 7 days

- **Day -7 to -3**: One final mock. Review thoroughly. Light formula sheet review. Re-read your Ethics summary notes.
- **Day -2**: Light review only. Re-read your weak-area summary cards. No new material. Confirm exam logistics (ID, calculator, exam center address).
- **Day -1**: Stop studying by noon. Sleep. Walk. Hydrate. Pack your bag. Don't try to learn anything new.
- **Day 0**: Eat a normal breakfast. Arrive 60 minutes early. Take the exam.

Cramming in the last 48 hours measurably hurts performance. The exam rewards rest.
